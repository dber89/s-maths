Topic 1 of 11
Read, Write, Order & Compare Numbers to 1,000,000
One million has 7 digits. Being confident with these numbers is the foundation of everything in place value.

📖 The six-digit number

Every number up to 999,999 has six places. We read them in groups of three:

Hundred ThousandsTen ThousandsThousandsHundredsTensOnes
473205

This is four hundred and seventy-three thousand, two hundred and five. We write it as 473,205.

📖 Ordering numbers

  • Always compare from the left-most digit first
  • If those digits are equal, move one place to the right
  • Use < (less than) and > (greater than) symbols
  • To order a list: find the smallest first, then the next, and so on

Worked Examples

Write in words: 308,070

→ Three hundred and eight thousand and seventy


Write in figures: "Sixty-two thousand, four hundred and nine"

→ 62,409


Order smallest to largest: 540,320  |  54,032  |  540,032  |  504,320

→ 54,032 < 504,320 < 540,032 < 540,320


Insert < or >: 389,450 __ 389,504

→ Both start 389,___ . Compare hundreds: 4 vs 5. So 389,450 < 389,504

Determine the Value of Each Digit in Numbers to 1,000,000
The digit 4 in 47,000 is worth 40,000 — its value depends entirely on its position.

📖 Digit vs. Value

The digit is just the symbol (0–9). The value is what it's worth in that position.

HThTThThHTO
638142

In 638,142, the digit 3 is in the ten-thousands position, so its value is 30,000.

📖 Partitioning

Any number can be split (partitioned) into its place values:

638,142 = 600,000 + 30,000 + 8,000 + 100 + 40 + 2

Worked Examples

What is the value of the 5 in 253,807?

→ 5 is in the ten-thousands column → value = 50,000


Partition 405,063:

→ 400,000 + 0 + 5,000 + 0 + 60 + 3


Which digit has the value 7,000 in 873,241?

→ Thousands column contains 3… wait, find 7: it's in the hundred-thousands? No — 8 is HTh, 7 is TTh → value = 70,000. The digit with value 7,000 would need a 7 in the Th column. Here there isn't one — a useful trick question!

Topic 3 of 11
Count Forwards and Backwards in Powers of 10
Multiplying or dividing by 10, 100, 1,000 etc. shifts digits left or right — digits don't change, positions do.

📖 Powers of 10

  • ×10 → shift all digits one place left
  • ×100 → shift all digits two places left
  • ×1,000 → shift all digits three places left
  • ÷10 → shift all digits one place right

Example: 3,400 × 100 = 340,000 (digits shift two places left)

📖 Counting in steps

Counting in 1,000s from 456,000:

455,000 → 456,000 → 457,000 → 458,000

Only the thousands digit changes — everything else stays the same.

Worked Examples

Count on 3 steps of 10,000 from 274,500:

→ 284,500 → 294,500 → 304,500


Count back 4 steps of 100 from 63,200:

→ 63,100 → 63,000 → 62,900 → 62,800


Find the missing number: 48,__ , 148,__ , 248,500, 348,500

→ Counting in steps of 100,000. Missing: 48,500 and 148,500

📖 Ordering negatives

Think of a thermometer. The lower the temperature, the colder (smaller) it is.

Order: −12, 3, −4, 0, −1

Topic 5 of 11
Round Whole Numbers to the Nearest 10, 100, 1,000, 10,000 and 100,000
Rounding makes numbers easier to work with and is a key skill in estimation and checking answers.

📖 The rounding rule

Look at the digit immediately to the right of where you're rounding:

  • If it's 0, 1, 2, 3 or 4 → round down (leave the rounding digit alone)
  • If it's 5, 6, 7, 8 or 9 → round up (add 1 to the rounding digit)
  • All digits to the right of the rounding place become 0

Worked Examples

Round 347,582 to the nearest 1,000:

→ Look at hundreds digit: 5 → round up → 348,000


Round 347,582 to the nearest 10,000:

→ Look at thousands digit: 7 → round up → 350,000


Round 347,582 to the nearest 100,000:

→ Look at ten-thousands digit: 4 → round down → 300,000


Round 347,582 to the nearest 10:

→ Look at ones digit: 2 → round down → 347,580

Topic 6 of 11
Solve Place Value Problems with Large Whole Numbers
11+ papers love tricky place value problems — missing digits, number machines, and "what am I?" puzzles.

📖 Types of place value problems

  • Missing digit: Find the unknown digit using clues
  • Number machines: Apply a sequence of operations
  • "I am a number": Read clues carefully and work systematically
  • Make the largest/smallest: Arrange given digits

Worked Examples

I have 6 digits. My thousands digit is 3. My ten-thousands digit is double my thousands digit. My hundred-thousands digit is half my ten-thousands digit. My hundreds digit is 0. My tens digit is 9. My ones digit is 5. What number am I?

→ Th=3, TTh=6, HTh=3, H=0, T=9, O=5 → 363,095


Make the largest possible 5-digit number using digits 3, 7, 1, 9, 4 (each once):

→ Arrange largest first: 97,431


4_,825 rounded to the nearest thousand is 48,000. What digit could _ be?

→ Rounding the thousands digit 4? to nearest 1000, look at hundreds (8) → always rounds up. But we get 48,000 so the ten-thousands digit must stay 4... meaning _ = 7 or 8 (giving 47,825 → 48,000 or 48,825 → 49,000). Only _ = 7 works.

Topic 7 of 11
Read Roman Numerals to 1,000 and Recognise Years in Roman Numerals
Roman numerals appear on clock faces, film credits, and building inscriptions — and in 11+ papers!

📖 The 7 symbols

SymbolIVXLCDM
Value1510501005001000

📖 The subtraction rule

  • IV = 4    IX = 9
  • XL = 40    XC = 90
  • CD = 400    CM = 900
  • A smaller symbol before a larger one means subtract
  • A smaller symbol after a larger one means add

Worked Examples

Convert MCMXCIV to a number:

→ M=1000, CM=900, XC=90, IV=4 → 1994

MCMXCIV = 1994

Write 2024 in Roman numerals:

→ 2000=MM, 24=XXIV → MMXXIV

MMXXIV = 2024

What year is MDCCCLXXXVIII?

→ M=1000, D=500, CCC=300, LXXX=80, VIII=8 → 1888

Topic 10 of 11
Round Whole Numbers to a Required Degree of Accuracy
Rounding to the nearest million or choosing the appropriate degree of accuracy for a context — a harder skill tested at top schools.

📖 Rounding large numbers

  • To nearest million: look at the hundred-thousands digit
  • To nearest 100,000: look at the ten-thousands digit
  • The rules are exactly the same — always look one place to the right

📖 Choosing accuracy

In real life, the degree of accuracy depends on context:

  • Population of a country → nearest million or 100,000
  • Distance to the Moon → nearest 1,000 km
  • Price of a house → nearest £1,000 or £10,000

Worked Examples

Round 6,847,293 to the nearest million:

→ Look at HTh digit: 8 → round up → 7,000,000


Round 6,847,293 to the nearest 100,000:

→ Look at TTh digit: 4 → round down → 6,800,000


A number rounded to the nearest 10,000 is 380,000. What is the smallest it could be?

→ 375,000 (halfway point rounds up, so 375,000 → 380,000)


And the largest?

→ 384,999

Topic 11 of 11
Use Negative Numbers in Context and Calculate Intervals Across Zero
Finding the difference between a positive and negative number, or between two negatives, is a classic 11+ question type.

📖 Calculating intervals (differences)

To find the difference between two numbers on either side of zero, count the steps:

From −5 to +3: count up from −5 to 0 (5 steps) then 0 to 3 (3 steps) = 8

Or use: difference = |a| + |b| when the numbers have opposite signs

|−5| + |3| = 5 + 3 = 8 ✓

📖 Two negatives

Difference between −8 and −3:

Count from −8 to −3 = 5 steps

Or: −3 − (−8) = −3 + 8 = 5

Worked Examples

The temperature drops from 4°C to −9°C. What is the change?

→ 4 to −9: go from 4 down to 0 (4 steps), then 0 to −9 (9 steps) = 13°C drop


What is the difference between −15 and 22?

→ 15 + 22 = 37


A submarine is at −45m. A fish swims at −12m. How far apart?

→ −12 − (−45) = −12 + 45 = 33m


The temperature at 6am was −7°C. By noon it had risen 11°. What was it at noon?

→ −7 + 11 = 4°C
